# Existing Marketing And Pricing Surfaces

Use this reference for an implemented landing, launch, portfolio, service, or pricing page. Preserve SEO, analytics, consent, forms, legal copy, routes, and approved brand assets unless explicitly in scope.

## Frame The Decision

Identify:

- offer: what is sold, launched, explained, or requested;
- audience: buyer, evaluator, developer, recruiter, operator, consumer, or mixed;
- conversion: signup, purchase, contact, demo, download, subscribe, or trust;
- proof: real product state, output, case, customer evidence, metric, security/compliance, or asset gap;
- objections: price, trust, complexity, migration, lock-in, maintenance, support, or time-to-value;
- current funnel: entry source, primary CTA, secondary path, and measurement hooks.

Do not assume the copy or section order is in scope. Diagnose whether the weakness is structure, evidence, hierarchy, visual identity, or interaction first.

## Landing Page Repair

Make the first viewport answer:

- What is this?
- Who is it for?
- Why should they trust it?
- What should they do next?

Keep the primary CTA visible on a realistic small laptop and mobile unless the page intentionally requires education first. Place real proof or a product artifact early enough to support the claim.

Give sections distinct persuasive jobs: problem, mechanism, workflow, proof, comparison, outcome, objection, FAQ, or final action. Avoid repeating one three-card pattern as the whole narrative.

Use imagery, type, palette, and one signature move derived from the actual product/brand. Do not fabricate dashboards, logos, testimonials, usage metrics, or operational metadata.

## Pricing Repair

Lock the model before styling cards:

- per-seat, usage, tiered, flat, freemium, enterprise/contact, or custom;
- monthly/annual, tax/currency, overage, discount, trial, cancellation, and hidden-fee behavior;
- comparison basis: features, limits, users, volume, support, compliance, or outcome;
- recommended-plan rule and intended buyer;
- enterprise or custom-quote path;
- risk reversal: migration, support, SLA, security, refund, or trial.

Align names, prices, periods, limits, CTAs, and feature rows for fast comparison. Use tabular numerals for changing prices when the typeface supports them. Keep the recommended plan distinct without obscuring cheaper or valid alternatives. Explain complex limits near the decision.

## Evidence And Trust

- Prefer real output, product screenshots, working demos, or verified customer evidence.
- Keep claims specific enough to verify.
- Preserve disclosures, legal qualifications, consent, and accessible form behavior.
- Keep security/compliance claims accurate and scoped.
- Treat social proof supplied only as placeholder content as a blocker, not permission to invent it.
- Verify analytics events after changing CTA structure or navigation.

## Visual And Responsive Proof

Inspect at minimum:

- first viewport at small laptop and mobile;
- first real proof/product section;
- pricing or primary decision point;
- final CTA/objection region;
- keyboard focus and form errors where relevant;
- long heading, translated copy, missing media, and narrow-card content.

Use same-state before/after or reference/after artifacts. A full-page screenshot helps narrative rhythm but does not replace viewport-level checks of hierarchy and fold.

## Completion

- The offer and next action are unambiguous.
- Real proof appears before decorative confidence.
- Each section does a different job.
- Pricing presents comparable information consistently and honestly.
- SEO, analytics, forms, legal copy, and accessibility remain intact or are intentionally changed and verified.
- Visual identity belongs to the subject rather than a stock template.
